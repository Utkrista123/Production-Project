1. Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB)
🔍 Symptoms
Yellowing of leaf tips that spreads downward
Water-soaked streaks along leaf margins
Leaves turn pale green → yellow → white
Drying and wilting of leaves (burnt appearance)
“Kresek” phase: sudden wilting of young plants
Milky bacterial ooze from cut leaves
Reduced tillering and stunted growth
🧪 Analysis (Cause & Conditions)
Caused by bacterium Xanthomonas oryzae
Spreads through:
Rain splash
Irrigation water
Wind
Favored by:
High humidity
Warm temperatures (25–34°C)
Heavy nitrogen fertilizer use
Enters through wounds or natural openings
🛡️ Prevention
Use resistant rice varieties
Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilizer
Maintain proper field drainage
Use clean, certified seeds
Practice crop rotation
Avoid overcrowding of plants
Remove infected plant debris
💊 Treatment
Apply copper-based bactericides
Use antibiotics like streptomycin (where allowed)
Spray neem-based bio-pesticides
Drain excess water temporarily
Apply silicon fertilizers to strengthen plants

2. Brown Spot
🔍 Symptoms
Small circular to oval brown spots on leaves
Spots have gray center and dark brown margins
Lesions may merge forming large patches
Infection on grains (discolored seeds)
Reduced grain quality and yield
Leaves dry prematurely
🧪 Analysis
Caused by fungus Bipolaris oryzae
Common in:
Nutrient-deficient soils
Drought conditions
Spread by:
Wind
Rain
Infected seeds
Often linked to poor soil fertility
🛡️ Prevention
Use disease-free seeds
Apply balanced fertilizers (especially potassium)
Maintain proper irrigation
Improve soil fertility
Avoid water stress
Seed treatment before sowing
💊 Treatment
Spray fungicides (e.g., mancozeb, propiconazole)
Seed treatment with fungicide
Apply foliar nutrients
Use organic compost to improve soil health

3. Leaf Blast
🔍 Symptoms
Diamond-shaped (spindle) lesions
Gray or white center with brown margins
Lesions enlarge and merge
Leaves dry and die
Neck blast causes panicle breakage
Reduced grain filling
🧪 Analysis
Caused by fungus Magnaporthe oryzae
Thrives in:
Cool nights and warm days
High humidity
Spread by airborne spores
Favored by:
Excess nitrogen
Dense planting
🛡️ Prevention
Grow resistant varieties
Avoid excessive nitrogen use
Maintain proper spacing
Ensure good air circulation
Timely planting
💊 Treatment
Apply fungicides:
Tricyclazole
Carbendazim
Spray at early stage
Use biological control agents (e.g., Trichoderma)

4. Leaf Scald
🔍 Symptoms
Large irregular lesions on leaves
Lesions start at leaf tips
Alternating light and dark bands (zoning)
Leaves appear scorched or burned
Premature drying of leaves
🧪 Analysis
Caused by fungus Microdochium oryzae
Occurs in:
High humidity
Poor nutrient conditions
Spread by:
Wind
Rain
Often appears in stressed plants
🛡️ Prevention
Maintain proper fertilization
Improve drainage
Use resistant varieties
Avoid plant stress
Ensure proper spacing
💊 Treatment
Apply fungicides like:
Thiophanate-methyl
Mancozeb
Remove infected leaves
Improve plant nutrition

5. Narrow Brown Spot
🔍 Symptoms
Narrow, linear brown streaks on leaves
Lesions run parallel to veins
Spots may enlarge and merge
Leaves become dry and weak
Reduced photosynthesis
🧪 Analysis
Caused by fungus Cercospora oryzae
Favored by:
High humidity
Warm temperatures
Spread by:
Wind
Rain
More common in nutrient-poor soils
🛡️ Prevention
Use resistant varieties
Maintain balanced fertilization
Avoid overcrowding
Improve field sanitation
Use clean seeds
💊 Treatment
Spray fungicides:
Mancozeb
Chlorothalonil
Apply micronutrients (especially zinc)
Remove infected plant debris
